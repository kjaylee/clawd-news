## The Framework: Why Most AI Prompts Fail

Bad prompt:
```
Write an email.
```

Good prompt:
```
[Context] I'm a startup founder requesting a meeting with an investor.
[Tone] Professional but warm.
[Format] Subject line + 3 paragraphs max.
[Include] Our recent milestone: 100K MAU, 300% YoY growth.
```

**The difference is specificity.** AI isn't psychic. Feed it constraints, get better outputs.

I call this the **CLEAR framework:**

| Letter | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **C** | Context | Who you are, situation |
| **L** | Language | Tone, style, formality |
| **E** | Expectation | Output format, length |
| **A** | Action | Exactly what to do |
| **R** | Refinement | Allow for iteration |

## Template 3: Email That Gets Responses

For cold outreach, I use this:

```markdown
Write a cold email for the following scenario:

[SENDER] Marketing manager at a B2B SaaS startup
[RECIPIENT] VP of Sales at a Fortune 500 company
[GOAL] Book a 15-minute intro call
[HOOK] We helped [similar company] increase sales efficiency by 40%

Requirements:
- Subject line: curiosity-driven, under 50 characters
- Opening line: personal or relevant insight (no "I hope this finds you well")
- Body: 3 sentences max, focus on THEIR problem
- CTA: specific ask with low friction
- Total length: under 100 words

Write 3 versions:
1. Problem-focused (pain point)
2. Social proof (results)
3. Curiosity-driven (question)
```

**Result:** My response rate went from 3% to 12%. That's 4x more conversations.

## Template 4: Debug Like a Senior Dev

When stuck on a bug:

```markdown
I have a bug in my [JavaScript/Python/etc] code.

[EXPECTED BEHAVIOR]
When user clicks submit, form data saves to database.

[ACTUAL BEHAVIOR]
Form submits but data doesn't persist. No errors in console.

[RELEVANT CODE]
{paste code}

[WHAT I'VE TRIED]
- Checked network tab (request shows 200 OK)
- Logged data object (looks correct)
- Verified database connection (other queries work)

Diagnose step by step:
1. First, identify possible causes
2. Then, explain the most likely issue
3. Finally, provide the fix with explanation
```

**Result:** Problems that took hours now take minutes. I learn *why* things break.

## Template 6: Learn Anything, Fast

For complex topics:

```markdown
Explain [quantum computing] in 3 levels:

**Level 1: ELI5 (Explain Like I'm 5)**
- Use simple analogies
- No jargon
- 2-3 sentences

**Level 2: College Student**
- Include basic technical concepts
- Define key terms
- 1 paragraph

**Level 3: Practitioner**
- Technical depth
- Real-world applications
- Code example if applicable
- 2-3 paragraphs

After each level, provide:
- 1 question I should ask next
- 1 common misconception to avoid
```

**Result:** I learn faster by understanding at multiple depths.

## Template 7: Content Repurposing Machine

Got one good blog post? Turn it into 10 pieces of content:

```markdown
Repurpose this content for multiple platforms:

[ORIGINAL CONTENT]
{paste your blog post or article}

Create:

1. **Twitter/X Thread** (10 tweets)
   - Hook in first tweet
   - One insight per tweet
   - End with CTA

2. **LinkedIn Post** (300 words)
   - Professional angle
   - Personal insight or story
   - Question to drive engagement

3. **Email Newsletter** (500 words)
   - Personal tone
   - "Reply to this email" CTA
   - P.S. with bonus tip

4. **YouTube Script Outline**
   - Hook (15 seconds)
   - 3 main points (2 min each)
   - Call to action

5. **Podcast Talking Points**
   - 5 discussion topics
   - Questions a host might ask
   - Stories to share
```

**Result:** One piece of content becomes a week of posts across platforms.
